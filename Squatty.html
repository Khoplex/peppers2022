Squatty

Description:

This is a great pepper. My ability to come up with good names for new strains is, well, horrible to say the least. I called this squatty because it has always been and extremely short plant for me. It has never grown above 2 feet tall yet, but tends to grow laterally a good amount. Even though small it is quite productive. This was born from some 7 Pot Orange seeds I received from another vendor years ago. These are more like a habanero and no where near the heat you would expect from a 7 pot. The pods to me are more of a yellow, but could also be a light orange to golden color. The pods tend to be a very nice size and have a really good heat, more than a normal habanero and a nice strong citrus flavor to them. These are one of my favorites and one I would likely grow every year if I was just growing for my own use. They have been quite stable since I first grew them years ago with minimal variation.

Specifics:

Species: Capsicum Chinense
Heat: Hot
